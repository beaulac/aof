The thought of a thing is less like the thing itself than a web of small reminders of how
it all seemed at the time. In the web presented here, Jumper’s perception is separated
into the many connected reminders1 that form his memory of the AoF seminar.

1. The circular nodes contain audio representations of Jumper’s reminders.
The Analysis of Failure seminar was not so neatly clarified in Mark Jumper’s mind.

If Jumper were asked to recount his experience conducting the AoF seminar
without the benefit of his tedious, meticulated lineation, it would come forth
from him more like this display of fragile pliability than his otherwise presented Reconstruction.

Here, we thoughtfully Reconduct his Analysis of Failure.

It is impressions, not facts, that spread around the various lobes and folds, each one
doing its part to retain something close to a whole.

As a result, the mind does not bother to remember things rigidly, but rather in a
malleable way, ripe for ceaseless reinterpretation, so that we might grow and not stagnate.

And Mark Jumper’s Reconstructions become infinite.

And so it is because Mark Jumper fills the bareness of this Archive with his singular
interpretations that we can know it to be only as true as it is incomplete.

Because One can only be achieved by combining All.
